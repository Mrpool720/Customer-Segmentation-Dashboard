Project Summary
Customer Segmentation using K-Means Clustering analyzes a marketing campaign dataset of 2216 customers to identify distinct behavioral segments for targeted marketing strategies.

Key Achievements:

Data Pipeline: Cleaned missing values, engineered 7 features (Age, Income, Total_Spending, purchase channels, digital engagement, recency)

Optimal Clustering: Used Elbow Method (K=6) and Silhouette Analysis for model selection

Business Insights: Identified 6 distinct segments with varying spending power, channel preferences, and engagement levels

Production Ready: Saved scaler and KMeans models for Streamlit deployment

Cluster Profiles (mean values):

Cluster 0: Mid-age (61), moderate income/spending professionals

Cluster 1: Young families (55), low spending, high recency

Cluster 2: Seniors (70), high income/premium spenders, low web visits

Cluster 3: Young low-spenders (51), budget-conscious

Cluster 4: Prime customers (59), high web/store purchases

Cluster 5: Young high-income (46), premium multichannel shoppers

Business Impact: Enables personalized marketing, channel optimization, and lifetime value prediction.
